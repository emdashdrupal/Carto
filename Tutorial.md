### Frequently Used QGIS Operations

#### Panning and Zooming the Map

Firstly, please make sure that you are enabling the ‚ÄúPan Map‚Äù mode ‚Äî You can found the button in the top toolbar, which features a glove or a raised hand (ü§ö) (Or go to View > Pan Map). Move your mouse to the canvas; left-click and hold, and drag the mouse in any direction to pan the map. To zoom in/out the map, scroll your mouse wheel forward/backward.

*Related QGIS documentation: [Navigating the Map Canvas](https://docs.qgis.org/3.34/en/docs/training_manual/basic_map/mapviewnavigation.html)*

#### Zooming Back After Getting Lost

Left-click on the ‚ÄúZoom Full‚Äù button in the top toolbar, which features a magnifier and three out-going arrows (üîé + ‚Üñ + ‚Üó + ‚Üò), to zoom back to the project‚Äôs canvas extent.

*Related QGIS documentation: [Exploring the map view](https://docs.qgis.org/3.34/en/docs/user_manual/map_views/map_view.html#exploring-the-map-view)*

#### Hide or Unhide the Layers

Left-click on the checkbox next to the layer‚Äôs name to hide/unhide the layer in the Layers panel. Alternatively, you can left-click on the layer‚Äôs name, and then left-click on the ‚ÄúManage Map Themes‚Äù button in the toolbar of the Layer panel, which features an opened eye (üëÅÔ∏è). You can select the relevant options including ‚ÄúShow All Layers,‚Äù ‚ÄúHide All Layers,‚Äù ‚ÄúShow Selected Layers,‚Äù ‚ÄúHide Selected Layers,‚Äù and ‚ÄúToggle Selected Layers‚Äù in the menu.

*Related QGIS documentation: [Configuring map themes](https://docs.qgis.org/3.34/en/docs/user_manual/introduction/general_tools.html#configuring-map-themes)*

#### Hide or Unhide the Labels

Sometimes you may want to toggle the labels to see the details underneath the text. You can achieve this by right-clicking on the layer‚Äôs name in the Layers panel, and then toggle the ‚ÄúShow Labels‚Äù option in the menu.

*Related QGIS documentation: [Overview of the context menu of the Layers panel](https://docs.qgis.org/3.34/en/docs/user_manual/introduction/general_tools.html#overview-of-the-context-menu-of-the-layers-panel)*

#### Adding External Maps

To add external maps like Google Maps or OpenStreetMap as the background in the canvas, navigate to the ‚ÄúXYZ Tiles‚Äù option in the Browser panel, which is on the left of the screen. You can left-click on the triangle (‚ñ∏) next to the icon to reveal its content; you can double-left-click on the ‚ÄúOpenStreetMap‚Äù option to add it to the canvas. Remember to adjust the order of the layers so that your city‚Äôs map is on top of the background layer. To add Google Maps to your map, right-click on the ‚ÄúXYZ Tiles‚Äù option, and then select the ‚ÄúNew Connection...‚Äù option. The XYZ Connection window will appear, where you can type in the desired connection name (e.g. *Google Maps*) and the URL to Google‚Äôs XYZ Tile server: `http://mt0.google.com/vt/lyrs=m&x={x}&y={y}&z={z}`. Click on the ‚ÄúOK‚Äù button when you are finished, and then double-left-click the connection you just created to add it to the map.

*Related QGIS documentation: [Using XYZ Tile services](https://docs.qgis.org/3.34/en/docs/user_manual/managing_data_source/opening_data.html#using-xyz-tile-services)*

#### Showing the Hidden Panels

If you accidentally close the panels, you can show them by right-clicking on any empty space of the toolbars on the top (or going to View > Panels) and then selecting the panels you need in the menu.

*Related QGIS documentation: [Panels](https://docs.qgis.org/3.34/en/docs/user_manual/introduction/general_tools.html#panels)*

#### Customizing the Symbology and the Labels of the Layers

To change the map‚Äôs appearance on demand, you can customize the style of the layers by yourself. Right-click on the layer‚Äôs name in the Layers panel, then select the ‚ÄúProperties...‚Äù option to show the Layer Properties window (or press F7 to show the Layer Styling panel). You can navigate to the Symbology tab or Labels tab to customize further based on Carto‚Äôs presets. You can find more tutorial and information on styling in QGIS‚Äôs documentation.

### Exporting Maps

After playing around with the settings, you may be happy with your current settings and want to export the map as images or PDFs. The simplist way is selecting ‚ÄúProject‚Äù, ‚ÄúImport/Export‚Äù, and ‚ÄúExport Map to Image...‚Äù (or ‚ÄúExport Map to PDF...‚Äù) options in order in the top menu bar. All you need to do is clicking on the ‚ÄúSave‚Äù button in the Save Map as Image window, and give your file a name. However, this method only exports the extent within your current canvas\*, and you can‚Äôt reuse the settings conveniently.

\* In fact, you *can* alter the extent settings in the Save Map as Image window, but I consider it more intuitive to do it in the Layout editor.
